About The Project

My team worked to develop an intuitive and informative website for The Wild Animal Sanctuary. We needed to find a way to help donors and volunteers find opportunities with a transparent and worthwhile organization.

Empathy & Research Definition & Ideation Prototyping & Testing Final Prototype Conclusion

Empathy & Research

Heuristic Evaluation

We reached out to our stakeholder and tried unsuccessfully to contact someone at the non-profit. We chose to gain insight and information about some of the unique problems that are being faced and how we may offer help by completing a heuristic evaluation on the website.

Interview Questions

We considered what we knew and what we would need to know to move forward. We felt we would gain the most information by asking questions focusing on previous experience volunteering and donating. Along with reasons behind making those decisions and what would prevent or increase future commitments. It was important to discover the drive behind our user.

Key Findings

Through our interviews we found that making a difference was really important behind the decision to volunteer or donate with an organization. Users talked about the importance of transparency in how funds and donations are being applied within an organization that is requesting help.

I like, I wish, What if?

We used this design thinking activity to structure and organize feedback and ideas when entering the prototyping phase. We were able to see right away some navigation ideas we needed to implement for usabilty ease to increass for our users in the future.

Empathy Map

We were able to gain a deeper insight into our users by gathering everything we learned during research. This empathy map captures all of the things that our primary user, says, thinks, does, and feels. We were able to add aditional observasions to the pains and gains at this pont as well.

Affinity diagram

We created an affinity diagram with the information from our user interviews and surveys. We found many similarities in the notes and observations gathered. Our potential users our passionate about where and who they volunteer and donate through.

Prioritization Matrix

Prioritizing easy access to donating and volunteering throughout the site increases the number of users. Volunteers who can access the information available when signing up and looking for donation opportunities can use their time more wisely at events.

User Persona

Katherine currently lives in downtown Denver. She has been Head of Operations for a few years now at a small startup. She grew up volunteering with her family and strongly believes in giving back to those in need. She is most passionate about organizations that align with the needs of the community, feeding the hungry and animal rescue.

Definition & Ideation

Problem Statement

During user interviews and analyzing survey data, we discovered that people enjoy donating and volunteering, but are deterred by a lack of transparency and an inconvenient processes on the organizations part.

The Solution:

Therefore, we believe that transparency and convenience is a priority when users are donating or volunteering and that we might be able to help if we create a streamlined donation/volunteering process through the organizations website. We might do this by reorganizing the navigation and reprioritizing the homepage to emphasize the organizations mission and ways users can help. Doing this will allow our product to increase the number of returning donors and remove the frustration that comes with giving back so users can focus on what they enjoy about helping.

Value Proposition

My organization The Wild Animal Sanctuary is developing an intuitive and informative website to help donors and volunteers find volunteer and donation opportunities with a transparent and worthwhile organization.
We’re better because we offer organized and intuitive ways to volunteer and donate.
We’re believable because we care about human and wildlife interactions above all else and we do what others won’t.

User Scenario

We were able to derermine some risks / emotions of our user:

  • Not being prepared when arriving to volunteer
  • Donating to/ volunteering for an organization that doesn’t fit her values
  • Wasting her time on an organization she isn’t passionate about
  • Wants to feel like she is making a difference in an animals life

Storyboard

Depicted in our storyboard it is easy to follow along on Katherine‘s journey as she discovers the wild animal sanctuary. Quickly we can see how important it is to our user to understand an organization that she will be aligning herself with. Being able to help the community and give back is a very rewarding process.

Competitor Analysis

We conducted a competitor analysis and we were able to deduce strengths and weaknesses in both our direct and indirect competitors. We were able to use the information to inspire the design layout and some weaknesses to showcase in our redesign.

User Flow & Task Flow

Relying on task flows and user flows we were able to determine what pages would hold what information and what pages would be most important. We were able to deduce that these seven pages were necessary to complete our user flows. We also found that we needed additional alerts, models and overlays to complete tasks.

Card Sorting & Sitemap

We did many iterations on our card sorting and site map. We focused on what was most important to our users based on interviews. Staying focused on simplifying navigation, transparency with funds, and easy access to information on donating and volunteering opportunities.

Prototyping & Testing

Sketches

When sketching out an idea for the first design, the focus was to keep the current content on the home page. Moving around the content helped to inspire some of the designs that were carried over into our mid and even full fidelity wireframes.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

Our main focus was condensing both the size of the website’s navigation and the number of pages contained in the website as a whole.

Mobile Design

When creating the design for the mobile layout, we wanted users to have the familiar feeling of our dest top design. By including small features in the design consistent with other moblie sites, also helps users easily navigate the pages of content without having to learn a new system.

UI Style Guide

For the UI, we wanted to create a warm and welcoming experience that engaged users looking to volunteer and donate but also had a childlike appeal for families wanting to visit the sanctuary.

Usability Testing

The importance of the interactions being minimal and simple are indicated in notes gathered from usability testing. Users were able to find volunteer options and ways to sign up for our newsletter with ease. Users liked the option for signing up at the bottom of the page.
